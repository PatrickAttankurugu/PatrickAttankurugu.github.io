AI in Agriculture: A Transformational Shift

I was born and 'baptized' into farming.

Growing up as a child,my daily life consisted of taking my father's cattle to the bush for grazing every three days. It was like a batton being passed on from one set of cow boys to the next set. One set would often follow the cattle up to a certain age, and then pass the batton to a younger set(we were a large family of one father, four mothers, 18 children and several grandchildren).
By age 11, when my father passed on, I was given a farmland and by age 17 when the cattle were distributed, I had one. I spent my days immersed in the soil of my farm. As I grew older, the fields, crops, and livestock that my early life was characterised by became an intrinsic part of my identity.
Whilst in Junior High, I would often wake up very early at dawn to send my cattle to the bush for grazing, and by about 8AM, I would bring them to the school park where they would lay down and I would go to sit in class and keep checking on them so they don't go to graze on someone's crops. That was what used to happen almost every three days for a number of years
By 2013, there were no cattle again in the family, it was also the year I wrote Basic Education Certificate Examination, emerging as the second best in the Bawku West District and best in my school since its inception more than 20 years before my birth. I subsequently gained admission to study General Science in Bolgatanga Senior High, and then later studied Computer Science at the University of Ghana.
It wasn't until recently that I began to see the agricultural industry in a new light.
I got inspired when co-founder and director of Trestle Academy, Robert Wonders posted on his whatsapp status about John Dumelo, a Ghanaian actor and one of Ghana's top farmers. After reading about him and agriculture in Ghana, I decided to begin a journey with Kosmos Innovation Center’s AgriTech Challenge. This challenge, a 7-month incubation program that seeks to foster entrepreneurship and innovation in the agricultural sector, opened my eyes to the potential of merging traditional farming practices with advanced technologies. The goal? To tackle some of the most pressing agricultural challenges of our time.

The Dawn of AI in Agriculture

Artificial Intelligence, or AI, has been making waves across various sectors, and agriculture is no exception. As the global population continues to rise, so does the need for efficient, sustainable farming practices. AI, with its ability to learn, reason, and process large volumes of data, holds the key to transforming traditional farming methods and meeting these burgeoning demands.

AI-based farming systems can use real-time data to track and predict various factors affecting the crop yield, such as weather conditions, pest infestation, soil quality, and crop health. This use of data analytics, driven by AI, is paving the way for more informed decision-making processes and ultimately, higher crop yield and quality.

A Personal Venture into AI in AgricTech

My transition from conventional farming practices to technology-aided agriculture has been an adventure, akin to a revelation and homecoming of sorts. The decision to apply AI to agriculture came after I read about the remarkable strides startups were making at the Kosmos Innovation Center. Inspired by their feats, I threw myself into the Kosmos Innovation Center’s AgriTech Challenge Classic 2023. Participating in this challenge has provided my team and me with an unparalleled opportunity to develop a sustainable and efficient business model, which is opening up a whole new world where the lines between agriculture and technology blur.

While our startup- PIGCASS is still in the incubation stage, the primary goal is clear – to create a symbiotic model that brings together the best aspects of cassava farming and pig rearing, fuelled by AI technology. More importantly, we are dedicated to creating organic products and reducing the carbon footprint of our farming practices, reflecting our commitment to sustainability and combating climate change.

The Potential of AI in Africa's Agricultural Landscape

Across Africa, agriculture forms the backbone of many economies, including Ghana. Despite its significance, the sector often grapples with issues like inefficient farming techniques, climate change impacts, and lack of access to modern technologies. Here is where AI can be a game-changer.

By integrating AI with farming, we can optimize the use of resources, boost crop yield, and develop resilience against climate change. For instance, predictive analytics, powered by AI, can aid farmers in making informed decisions about crop rotation and hybrid seed usage, which could drastically enhance productivity and sustainability.

AI-driven farming equipment and machinery could also help reduce human labor, improve efficiency and precision, and ultimately contribute to a more sustainable agricultural sector. Given the urgency of these challenges and the potential of AI, it's clear that the intersection of AI and agriculture in Africa is a frontier ripe for exploration.

My Journey Continues...

My journey into AI and agriculture is just beginning, and I am excited about the transformational potential it holds. The insights and experiences gained from my participation in the Kosmos Innovation Center AgriTech Challenge Classic 2023 so far have been invaluable, and I am thrilled to continue exploring and innovating in this space.

Together with my team, we're focusing on creating a scalable and sustainable business model. Through our AI-driven systems, we hope to address key challenges in the agricultural sector, increase crop yield, and boost the economy, while staying true to our commitment to sustainability and social impact.

The future of agriculture in Africa is at the cusp of a significant transformation, and I am enthusiastic about being part of this journey. Together, I believe we can harness the power of AI to revolutionize agriculture and make a meaningful contribution to our world.
